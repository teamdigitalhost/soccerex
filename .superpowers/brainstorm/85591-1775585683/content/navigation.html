How should the navigation accommodate the new verticals?

You're adding Deal Network, HerSoccerex, The Pitch, and possibly Digital Infrastructure. The nav needs to hold all of this without feeling cluttered.

A

Flat Nav with "Platform" Dropdown

Top-level: EVENTS · ABOUT · PLATFORM ▾ · INSIGHTS · GET IN TOUCH

PLATFORM dropdown reveals: Deal Network, HerSoccerex, The Pitch, Digital Infrastructure. Clean top bar, one extra click for verticals. The dropdown signals "there's more here."

Pros

  • Keeps nav clean (5 items)
  • Groups verticals logically
  • Scales if you add more verticals

Cons

  • Verticals hidden behind a click
  • Mobile dropdown-in-dropdown is tricky
B

Mega Menu

Top-level: EVENTS · ABOUT · WHAT WE DO ▾ · INSIGHTS · GET IN TOUCH

"WHAT WE DO" opens a full-width mega menu with all four verticals shown as cards (icon + title + one-liner). Also includes links to Global Network and Gallery. Premium, editorial feel.

Pros

  • All verticals visible at once
  • Room for descriptions, not just labels
  • Feels like a platform, not just a site

Cons

  • More complex to build
  • Can feel heavy on smaller screens
C

Flat Nav, All Visible

Top-level: EVENTS · DEAL NETWORK · HERSOCCEREX · THE PITCH · ABOUT · INSIGHTS · GET IN TOUCH

Everything top-level, no dropdowns. Simple, every page one click away. But 7 items is a lot for a nav bar and the vertical names are long.

Pros

  • Zero hidden pages
  • Maximum visibility for new products
  • Simple implementation

Cons

  • 7+ items crowds the nav
  • Long names ("HerSoccerex") eat space
  • Doesn't scale

💡 My recommendation: A (Flat + dropdown) — keeps the nav clean while clearly signaling that Soccerex is more than events. The dropdown label "Platform" itself communicates the Soccerex 2.0 positioning.
