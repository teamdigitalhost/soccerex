Home Page: What's the scroll story?

The current homepage leads with the 30th anniversary hero, then the Europe 2026 event, then content sections. We need to inject the Soccerex 2.0 platform story. Where does it go?

A

Hero → Event → Platform → Verticals → Social Proof → CTA

Keep the current hero + Europe 2026 block, then insert a new "Soccerex 2.0" section with the mission statement + 5 platform pillars, followed by a 3-card vertical showcase (Deal Network, HerSoccerex, The Pitch), then existing social proof/testimonials, then CTA.

The event stays the hook. The platform story earns its place after you've shown what's happening now.

B

Hero → Platform → Verticals → Event → Social Proof → CTA

Update the hero to lead with the new mission ("Fueling the Global Growth of the Football Community"), then immediately showcase the platform pillars and 3 verticals. The Europe 2026 event block drops to section 3. Social proof and CTA follow.

Platform-forward. The event is important but the transformation leads. Signals that Soccerex is bigger than any single event.

C

Hero (split) → Verticals → Event → Stats → Social Proof → CTA

The hero itself does double duty: left side has the anniversary headline, right side has a compact "What's New" card linking to the 3 verticals. Immediately below the hero, the verticals get their own showcase section. Then the event, then stats and social proof.

No scrolling needed to discover the new direction. Both stories coexist in the first viewport.

💡 My recommendation: B — with the evolution framing (Option A from earlier), the hero should acknowledge the legacy but the FIRST thing you scroll into should be the platform story. If the event leads, visitors think "this is still just an events company." The platform section right after the hero says "we've evolved."
